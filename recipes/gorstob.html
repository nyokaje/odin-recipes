Gorstob

Description

Gorstob is named for the first letters of its common ingredients: ground beef, onion, rice, salsa/spices/spinach, tomatoes, and black beans (deepest apologies that "O" is in there twice for only one "O" ingredient). A flexible recipe based on canned tomatoes and canned black beans, add whatever additional ingredients you feel up to.

Lack the will to live? Heat up the tomatoes and beans, add some spices. Not sad enough to consider that a meal? Saute onion, brown some ground beef, cook rice, and then dump the rest of the ingredients in to create a weird stew that contains protein, carbs, and vegetables! They may be mostly canned vegetables, but that still counts for something. Sprinkling nutritional yeast on top will add even more protein, as will consuming over some form of egg.

Ingredients

  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 1 can black beans
  • 1 pound ground beef
  • 1 can spinach
  • 1 C rice
  • 1 onion
  • 1 jar of green salsa
  • Desired spices (e.g. pepper flakes, garlic, onion)

Steps

  1. Get rice started using desired rice cooking method. The rice is generally the most annoying part if making on the stovetop because it takes a while to cook and needs babysitting, so if you only have one pot things will be very annoying. Rice cookers are cool.
  2. Chop onions into desired shape and saute on stovetop (I guess oil for sauteing the onions is the last "O"!). If you're being fancy and using fresh spinach (or other greens), then you'll probably want to add it at the end of the onions' cooking time to soften it up too.
  3. Brown ground beef on stovetop until it is safe for consumption (you need to figure out when that is, I generally overcook it so cannot help you there). Can wait until onions are done and reuse that pan.
  4. Combine onions, beef, spinach, diced tomatoes, drained black beans, and spices in pot and heat up on stovetop. If you're really hurting for space and equipment, you can do the onions and beef in the same big pot and then combine everything in that one pot. Or, if you're hurting for time, you can start warming up the tomatoes and beans while the onions and beef are cooking.
  5. Wait until the stew is hot, or simmer longer to boil off some water and make a thicker stew.
  6. Mix with rice and put green salsa on top (if desired) to eat :)
