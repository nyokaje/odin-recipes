Seitan

Description

Basic homemade seitan is easier to make than you would think, and if you don't mind the rubbery, slightly globulous texture (or actually enjoy it) then this weirdly-protein-rich staple is for you. Made with wheat gluten, cut with another kind of flour-like ingredient (wheat flour or cheakpea flour, for instance), just add water to get the basic dough. Vegetable stock and soy sauce provide the simmering sauce and flavor. Make the dough and get the broth simmering, then enjoy an hour paying just a little attention to the stovetop to get the final product.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/4 C wheat gluten
  • 1/3 C other flour (whole wheat flour, chickpea flour, etc.)
  • 1 1/4 C water
  • ~50 oz vegetable stock
  • 1 C soy sauce
  • Desired spices (e.g. pepper flakes, garlic, pepper)

Steps

  1. Combine vegetable stock, soy sauce, and spices in pot and bring to a simmer
  2. While waiting for broth to reach a simmer, mix wheat gluten and other flour of choice in bowl and mix
  3. Add water to flour mix and knead for 5 minutes
  4. Let dough sit for 5 minutes
  5. Cut dough into desired number of pieces (5 or 6 works well but smaller pieces are possible) and add to broth
  6. Simmer (do not let come to boil) in broth for 1 hour, turning pieces over halfway through
  7. Eat, or keep in refrigerator by storing seitan in the broth in a closed container (will keep for less than a week)
